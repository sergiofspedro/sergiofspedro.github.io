Regenerative Potential Index – An Original Evaluation Framework Applied to Amsterdam

2024 · PhD Research · Management PhD

The Regenerative Potential Index (RPI) is an original evaluation framework developed as part of PhD research to measure and compare the regenerative potential of urban territories. Unlike existing sustainability indices, the RPI is designed to be applicable to both public and private sectors and asks a forward-looking question: how capable is a territory of regenerating itself? It aggregates multiple indicators across four dimensions — Economic, Environmental, Political and Social — into a single composite score (0–100) per territorial unit per year.

The RPI framework introduces a novel methodological approach combining dimension weighting, z-score normalisation, and temporal aggregation to produce a score comparable across geographies and over time. Its design explicitly targets the gap between static sustainability audits and dynamic regenerative capacity assessment — making it equally usable for urban planning authorities, real-estate developers, impact investors, and policy evaluators.

This case study applies the RPI to the eight districts of the municipality of Amsterdam over a decade of data (2015–2024). The municipality's overall RPI in 2024 stands at 47.93, down from 49.98 in 2015. This modest decline conceals important contrasts: the Environmental dimension improved (+1.46 pts) while Economic performance deteriorated sharply (–7.21 pts, reaching 43.01 in 2024). MiningEurope – Water Conflicts at Portuguese Mining Sites (2015–2026)

2026 · Data Analysis · Friends of the Earth Europe (NGO)

MiningEurope is a data analysis project developed in collaboration with Friends of the Earth Europe, tracking water-related environmental conflicts at 14 mine sites across Portugal between 2015 and 2026. The project monitors newspaper coverage, legal disputes, civic engagement events, and contamination/depletion incidents for each site, producing eight interactive visualisations that together form a comprehensive environmental conflict intelligence dashboard.

The dataset covers active operations (Neves-Corvo, Panasqueira), contested projects (Barroso lithium mine — the most monitored site, peaking at 56 articles in 2024), and abandoned sites (Lousal, Urgeiriça). The analysis focuses on water impacts across Portugal's major river basins (Douro, Tagus, Sado, Mira, Cavado, Tamega), tracking both contamination and depletion conflicts over time.

LOOP – A Civic Participation Gateway for Every EU Citizen

2026 · EU Commission Civic Tech Hackathon · Brussels

LOOP is an open-source civic participation gateway built for the EU Commission Civic Tech Hackathon 2026. It does not replace existing official consultation platforms — it makes them discoverable, understandable, and actionable for every EU citizen, including the approximately 40–45 million people without a smartphone who are unreachable by any existing civic tech platform.

Citizens interact through the channel that works for them: a voice call (DTMF keypad voting, no smartphone needed), or a Telegram bot with inline keyboards and AI-powered free-text opinion submission. Both channels share the same backend — a single SQLite vote database — so votes cast by phone are immediately visible in the Telegram interface and vice-versa.

Each consultation is presented as a four-field card: title, theme, deadline, and an AI-generated plain-language summary. Citizens choose from four responses — Know More, Express My Opinion, Vote YES, or Vote NO. The Telegram bot sends free-text opinions to DeepSeek V4 Flash via OpenRouter for empathetic acknowledgement. All API keys are managed through Infisical (open-source secrets manager) — never hardcoded, injected at container startup. The full stack (Caddy, Docker, Flask, Telnyx, GitHub Actions) is open-source.

LOOP directly addresses the feedback loop absence identified as the primary driver of EU electoral abstention (49.3%): every contribution triggers an immediate confirmation and a live tracking page showing each consultation's status from Open → Under Review → Decision Published → Implementation.

System Architecture

Two Docker containers (Telegram bot and Voice bot) share a SQLite volume on a Hetzner VPS. Caddy handles HTTPS for Telegram webhooks; Telnyx provides the PSTN phone number. Secrets are injected at startup via Infisical.
